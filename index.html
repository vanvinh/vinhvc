Philosophy

The right name is not a detail.
It is an asymmetric advantage.

I
Strong names create leverage.

A great domain reduces your CAC, accelerates trust, and travels without explanation. It does marketing while you sleep. Most founders treat naming as a checkbox. The best ones treat it as infrastructure.

II
Digital identity compounds.

As categories mature, memorable digital real estate becomes harder to acquire. Premium domain assets appreciate as scarcity becomes undeniable. Owning the right name today, before the category matures, is one of the highest-upside positions available.

III
Simplicity outlasts trends.

Designs age. Names linger. The single-word domain, the one-line logo, the white space that says more than a paragraph. These are clarity as a competitive moat, not minimalism as aesthetics.
